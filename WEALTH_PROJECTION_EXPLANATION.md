# Wealth Projection & Tax Calculation Logic (2026 Rules)

This document provides a detailed explanation of the logic, tax structures, and retirement rules implemented in `calculations.py` for the German Wealth & Retirement Architect tool.

## Overview
The simulation is executed year-by-year from the user's `current_age` to their `end_age`. Calculations are performed in **nominal terms** (accounting for inflation) but are recorded and reported in **real terms** (purchasing power relative to today) to provide intuitive insights.

---

## 1. Statutory Pension (GRV)
The state pension logic accurately simulates the accumulation of *Entgeltpunkte* (EP) and the payout phase based on 2026 regulations.

*   **Accumulation Phase (Working Years):**
    *   While employed, the user's gross salary is divided by the provisional average yearly earnings of **€51,944** to calculate the EP gained per year.
    *   Earnings are capped at the *Beitragsbemessungsgrenze* (BBG) of **€101,400**.
    *   Partial retirement (`do_partial_ret`) is supported, allowing the user to earn a reduced salary between an early retirement age and a final retirement age, accumulating proportional EP.
*   **Payout Phase (Age 67 onwards):**
    *   The state retirement age is hardcoded to 67.
    *   The payout is calculated using the provisional 2026 *Rentenwert* of **€42.52** per month per EP.
    *   Formula: `Annual Pension = Total EP * 42.52 * 12 * Deflator`.

## 2. Private Pension Insurance
The private pension simulation is split into three distinct phases and utilizes the **12/62 Rule (Halbeinkünfteverfahren)** for taxation.

### Phases
*   **Phase 1 (Until Age 50 - Active Accumulation):**
    *   Monthly contributions are made.
    *   Contributions are reduced by a *Contribution Fee* (default 0.50%).
    *   The accumulated balance grows by the pre-retirement return rate but is reduced by an annual *Balance Fee* (default 0.22%).
*   **Phase 2 (Age 50 to 62 - Passive Growth):**
    *   Contributions stop.
    *   The capital continues to grow at the market rate, minus the annual balance fee.
*   **Phase 3 (Age 62 to 85 - Annuity Payout):**
    *   The capital is converted into an annuity payout until age 85.
    *   The payout is calculated using the net return rate to exhaust the capital exactly at age 85.

### Taxation (12/62 Rule)
When the payout begins, only the gains are taxed, with a special exemption because the contract runs past age 62 and lasts longer than 12 years:
1.  **Gain Calculation:** Gross Payout minus the proportion of the original contributions (basis).
2.  **Partial Exemption:** A 15% partial exemption is applied.
3.  **Halbeinkünfteverfahren:** Only 50% of the remaining gain is considered taxable income.
4.  This taxable gain is then added to the user's total taxable income and taxed at their personal income tax rate.

## 3. Stock Market (Depot) & Vorabpauschale
The stock market simulation uses a precise lot-based accounting system to calculate taxes, including *Vorabpauschale* and the *FIFO* (First-In, First-Out) rule.

### Contributions and Accumulation
*   Contributions are made monthly until the user reaches `early_retirement_age`.
*   A new "lot" is created for each year's contributions. This is critical for the FIFO simulation.
*   **ETF Switches:** The tool simulates switching the ETF being contributed to. By having multiple ETFs (`etf_switches`), the user can optimize taxes during retirement by selling the youngest ETF first, bypassing strict FIFO across the entire portfolio.

### Vorabpauschale (Advance Tax)
Calculated annually on December 31st based on 2026 rules:
*   **Basiszins:** 3.20%
*   **Basisertrag:** `Start Value * 3.20% * 0.70`
*   The actual `Vorabpauschale` is the minimum of the `Basisertrag` and the actual value gain in that year.
*   **Teilfreistellung:** 30% of this amount is tax-free (assuming equity funds).
*   **Sparerpauschbetrag:** The €1,000 allowance is applied against this amount first.
*   **Tax:** The remainder is taxed at **26.375%** (Abgeltungsteuer + Solidaritätszuschlag).
*   **Accounting:** The paid Vorabpauschale is deducted from the lot's value, and the lot's tax basis is increased to prevent double taxation upon final sale.

### Withdrawals
*   During retirement, stock withdrawals are calculated automatically to cover the *shortfall* (the gap between the target net income and the net income generated by state/private pensions).
*   Because taxes and health insurance depend on the gross withdrawal, a **binary search algorithm** is used to find the exact gross stock withdrawal needed to yield the required net shortfall.
*   **Capital Gains Tax on Withdrawal:** When a lot is sold, the gain is `Withdrawal Amount - Basis`. 30% Teilfreistellung is applied, the remaining Sparerpauschbetrag is utilized, and the rest is taxed at 26.375%.

## 4. Health & Long-Term Care Insurance (GKV/PV)
The simulation rigorously applies statutory health insurance rules based on the user's employment state and retirement age.

*   **Beitragsbemessungsgrenze (BBG):** Contributions are capped at an income of **€69,750** per year.
*   **Mindesteinkommen:** A minimum assessable income of **€14,140** per year applies to voluntarily insured individuals.

### Scenarios
1.  **Employed (Before Early Retirement):** GKV is deducted as part of the payroll net calculation; only the employee portion is considered implicitly. (In the tax logic, a flat 10% deduction is applied to salary for tax-base calculation).
2.  **Privatier (Early Retirement to Age 67):**
    *   The user is considered voluntarily insured in the GKV.
    *   **All income** is assessed: Private Pension payouts + Stock Market gains (not the principal withdrawal).
    *   The full GKV/PV rate (approx. 17.5% + PV) is applied up to the BBG.
3.  **State Retirement (Age 67+):**
    *   **KVdR (Krankenversicherung der Rentner):** If the user qualifies, they only pay health insurance on their state pension. Furthermore, they only pay half the KV rate (the state pays the other half) and the full PV rate. Private pensions and stock gains are completely **free** from GKV contributions.
    *   **Voluntary Insurance (if KVdR is not met):** The user continues to pay the full GKV/PV rate on **all** income streams (State Pension, Private Pension, Stock Gains) up to the BBG.

## 5. Income Tax (ESt) Calculation
The function `calc_income_tax_2026` mirrors the German progressive tax system, specifically targeting the projected 2026 brackets.

*   **Grundfreibetrag (Tax-Free Allowance):** €12,336
*   **Progressive Zones:**
    *   Zone 1 (Up to €17,005): Initial progressive rate.
    *   Zone 2 (Up to €66,760): Main progressive rate.
    *   Zone 3 (Up to €277,825): Flat 42% (Spitzensteuersatz).
    *   Zone 4 (Above €277,825): Flat 45% (Reichensteuer).
*   **Application:** The total taxable income (Gross Salary + State Pension + Taxable portion of Private Pension - GKV costs - Salary deductions) is calculated in nominal terms. The resulting tax burden is proportionally allocated back to the Salary, State Pension, and Private Pension streams for accurate accounting in the final output.
